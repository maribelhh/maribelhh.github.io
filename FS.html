Food Security and School Performance

Food security categories defined by U.S. Department of Agriculture are indicators of getting sufficient and nutritious food. Marginal/High Food Security, no problems or little changes in diets or food intake. Low food security, reports a reduce in the quality, variety, or desirability of diet, but little or no reduction of food intake. Finally, very low food security is an indicator of disrupted eating patterns and reduced food intake.

According to UN FAO, food security is related to having a normal growth and developing an active and healthy life. Here, visualization of association between food security categories and federal aid use is presented, also, relation between food security and school performance is explored. Differences by race and gender are included for food security and for school performance. School performace is measured as difficulty concentrating and delaying degree completation.

The University of Texas at El Paso (UTEP) has been collecting data on food and housing security of UTEP students since 2019 and continues to this day, data from 2022 is used in this project.

Summary

  • 74 percent of the students included in the data, were classified into very low food security.
  • 90% of the students were hispanics. Hispanics use federal loans in a higher rate than non-hispanics.
  • 37% of students into the very low FS category, reported difficulties concetrating almost everyday and; 22% had delayed degree completation for two semester or more. For high security students this percentages were 11% and 6% respectively.
  • Lower the food security category, higher the percentage of students delaying the degree completation or thinking about do it.
  • Non-hispanics reported having difficulties concetrating more often than hispanics.
  • Hipanics stopped out in a higher rate than non-hispanics.

How is use of government federal aid/assistance associated with food insecurity as measured by the USDA index categories?

In this section
  • Association between food security and federal aid.
  • Food security category and federal aid used by gender.
  • Food security category and federal aid by ethnicity.

Association between food security and federal aid.

74% of the students were classified into the very low FS category. The distribution of the federal aid used looks similiar across the USDA food categories. However, marginal/high FS students used loans in a higher rate than the rest.

Work-study is the aid most used and loans is the second one most used.

Food security category and federal aid used by gender

67% of the students were female. Males used work-study in higher rate than females and loans percentage is higher for females than males.

Food security category and federal aid by ethnicity

About 90% of the students were hispanics. There are differences in the distribution of federal aid by ethnicity.

In the low FS category, the first two most used programs were the same for both groups, however the third one was scholarship for hispanics and other aid for non hispanics.

The difference is bigger for students in the very low category, 89% of non-hispanics used work-study versus 48% of hispanics.

Does food insecurity (as measured by USDA index categories) have a relationship with the items pertaining to concentration on school and degree progress/completion?

In this section
  • Association between food security and concentration.
  • Association between food security and thinking of delaying degree completation.
  • Association between food security and delaying degree completation.

Association between food security and concentration

Very low FS students are having difficulties concentrating more often than the rest of the students. In particular 37% of the very low FS students reported everyday difficulties vs 11% of the marginal/high FS group.

In the other hand, 39% of the students in the marginal/high FS group indicated that never had difficulties concetrating, only 7% of the very low FS students answered the same.

Association between food security and thinking of delaying degree completation

Most of the marginal/high FS or low FS categories never thought about delaying degree completation, only 26% of the students in the very low category picked the same answer.

27% percent of the students in the very low category often thought about delaying degree completation, this rate is more than 4 times higher than the rate reported for marginal/high FS students.

Association between food security and delaying degree completation

94% of the students in the marginal/high FS category never stopped out from school. 37% of very low FS students stopped out by at least one semester.

Association between gender/race and thinking of delaying degree completation

Frequency of thinking of delaying distributions look similar across gender and etnicity groups.

Association between food security and delaying degree completation

Female stopped out in higher rate than males (36% vs 28%).

The difference is bigger between hispanics and non-hipanics. 35% of the hispanics stopped at least one semester vs 17% of the non-hispanics.
